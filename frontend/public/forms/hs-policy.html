Policy Statement
Urban Tree Services Ltd is absolutely committed to providing and maintaining a safe, healthy working environment for all directors, employees, subcontractors, clients, and members of the public affected by our work. The health, safety, and wellbeing of our people is our first and non-negotiable priority — it takes precedence over production, scheduling, and cost at all times.

We acknowledge that arboricultural work carries serious inherent risk — work at height, chainsaw use, working near power lines, and operating heavy plant. We manage these risks through disciplined hazard identification, generic Safe Work Method Statements (SWMS) aligned to ArbAus MIS 01–14, and a job-specific Site-Specific Safety Plan (SSSP) completed before every job. Every worker has the right and the responsibility to stop work if they believe conditions are unsafe.

This policy meets the requirements of the Health and Safety at Work Act 2015 and aligns with SiteWise Green Star grading expectations. It is reviewed annually and communicated to all workers at induction and at toolbox meetings.
Josh Micallef — Director, Urban Tree Services Ltd
1 Scope & Application

This policy applies to:

  • All permanent, casual, and part-time employees of Urban Tree Services Ltd
  • All subcontractors and labour-hire workers performing work on behalf of Urban Tree Services Ltd

All workers, regardless of employment status or tenure, must read, understand, and comply with this policy and the SSSP for each job before commencing work. SWMS are reviewed monthly at toolbox meetings — not required to be signed at every job.

3 Hazard Identification, Risk Assessment & Control

Urban Tree Services Ltd identifies and manages hazards using the hierarchy of control (HSWA s30):

  • Eliminate — remove the hazard entirely (e.g. change the work method)
  • Substitute — replace the hazard with a lower-risk alternative
  • Isolate — separate the hazard from workers and public (exclusion zones, barriers, lockout)
  • Engineering controls — physical controls: rigging systems, equipment guards, EWPs
  • Administrative controls — SWMS, SSSP, toolbox meetings, permits, supervision, scheduling
  • PPE — last line of defence; must meet the standard specified in the applicable SWMS
Risk rating method: Likelihood (1–5) × Consequence (1–5) = Risk Score. LOW (1–4) · MEDIUM (5–9) · HIGH (10–16) · CRITICAL (17–25). All hazards rated HIGH or CRITICAL must have additional controls applied before work proceeds.

Hazard identification occurs at two levels:

  • Generic — our 13 activity-based SWMS documents (SWMS-001 to SWMS-013) cover hazards common to each arboricultural activity, aligned with ArbAus MIS 01–14 and NZArb best practice
  • Site-specific — the SSSP, completed before every job, identifies hazards specific to the individual site: overhead utilities, ground conditions, proximity to structures, third-party presence, traffic, and weather

6 Working Near Power Lines ECP 34 · SWMS-011

Contact with live power lines is an extreme-consequence risk. The following minimum exclusion distances are mandatory at all times:

  • ≥ 4 metres from LV distribution lines (≤110kV)
  • ≥ 6 metres from HV transmission lines (>110kV)

These distances apply to workers, ropes, tools, plant, and any equipment. If the required distance cannot be maintained:

  • Stop work immediately
  • Contact the line owner (Powerco / Wellington Electricity / Lines Company) and arrange a line shutdown or approved insulation covering before resuming
  • Under no circumstances attempt to work within the exclusion zone without written line-owner approval
All power lines are treated as live at all times, regardless of appearance, de-energisation claims, or advice from third parties. No exceptions.
7 Working at Height SWMS-001 · SWMS-002 · WorkSafe NZ
  • Aerial rescue trained second person mandatory: All work above 3 metres must have an aerial-rescue trained crew member present on site at all times — WorkSafe NZ requirement
  • Climbers maintain two independent anchor points (IAP) in the canopy at all times (SRS / MRS)
  • All climbing systems inspected before each use per AS/NZS 4143.3; retired on any sign of damage
  • Arborist climbing saddle / harness rated to minimum 22kN (manufacturer spec)
  • Aerial rescue kit on site for all elevated work: ascender, controlled descent device (≥25kN), rescue lanyard
  • Aerial rescue drills practised by all climbing crew at minimum annually
8 Incident, Injury & Near-Miss Reporting

All incidents, injuries, and near-misses — regardless of severity — are reported to the Director immediately and documented using the Urban Tree Services Incident Report form.

Notifiable events under HSWA 2015 ss28–29 must be reported to WorkSafe NZ immediately:

  • Death of any person
  • Notifiable injury / illness: hospitalisation, amputation, loss of sight, serious head or spinal injury, serious burn or laceration, loss of a bodily function, serious illness
  • Notifiable incident: unplanned release of energy that could have caused a notifiable injury (e.g. line contact, fall from height, uncontrolled structural failure)
WorkSafe NZ 24-hour notifiable events line: 0800 030 040
The scene must not be disturbed after a notifiable event (except to assist the injured person or prevent further harm) until WorkSafe advises. Preserve evidence.

Near-misses are treated as serious learning opportunities. All near-misses are reviewed at the next toolbox meeting and trigger a review of the applicable SWMS.

13 Drug & Alcohol Policy

Urban Tree Services Ltd operates a strict drug and alcohol-free workplace. The safety-critical nature of arboricultural work — including chainsaw operation, aerial climbing, and traffic management — means impairment of any kind presents an unacceptable risk to life.

Our Rules

  • No worker may attend work, operate machinery, or climb while under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or any substance that impairs judgement, coordination, or reaction time
  • The legal blood alcohol limit for driving (50mg/100ml) applies as a minimum — we expect zero alcohol for anyone operating chainsaws, chippers, or working at height
  • Prescription or over-the-counter medications that may cause drowsiness, dizziness, or impaired coordination must be disclosed to the director before work. Workers may be redeployed to non-safety-critical tasks until the course of medication is complete
  • Workers are not to return to work within 8 hours of consuming alcohol

Testing

  • Urban Tree Services reserves the right to require drug and alcohol testing where there are reasonable grounds to suspect impairment, following a workplace incident or near-miss, or as part of a post-incident investigation
  • Testing may be conducted by a third-party provider. A positive result, refusal to test, or failure to comply with this policy is grounds for disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal
  • Workers who believe they may have a problem are encouraged to seek support confidentially through their GP or the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP). The company will support access to treatment without prejudice where the worker comes forward voluntarily before an incident occurs

Reporting

  • Any worker with concerns about a colleague's fitness for work must report this to the director or crew lead immediately — this is a safety obligation, not a disciplinary act
  • Workers sent home for impairment will not be paid for hours not worked
14 Fatigue Management

Fatigue impairs judgement and reaction time to a degree comparable to alcohol. Urban Tree Services recognises fatigue as a serious hazard given the physical demands of the work and the use of dangerous machinery.

  • Workers must arrive at work adequately rested. A minimum of 8 hours off duty (including reasonable sleep) between shifts is expected
  • Maximum working hours are 10 hours on-tool per day. Breaks of at least 30 minutes are required every 4 hours
  • Workers must advise their crew lead if they feel too fatigued to safely operate machinery or carry out climbing work. Redeployment to ground-based or administrative tasks may be arranged
  • Overtime and extended hours must be approved by the director. The health and safety of the worker takes priority over job completion timelines
  • Workers undertaking long-distance driving to/from remote sites should not also operate chainsaws or climb on the same day where this can be avoided
15 Code of Conduct

All Urban Tree Services workers are expected to conduct themselves professionally at all times — on site, while driving company vehicles, and when communicating with clients, contractors, and the public.

Expected Standards

  • Professionalism: Treat clients, members of the public, and colleagues with courtesy and respect at all times. Represent the company positively
  • Honesty: Workers must be truthful in all dealings with the company, clients, and WorkSafe NZ. Falsification of H&S records, timesheets, or incident reports is grounds for immediate dismissal
  • Punctuality: Arrive on time for agreed start times. Notify the crew lead as early as possible if you will be late or absent
  • Appearance: Wear clean, appropriate workwear and all required PPE while on site or travelling to/from jobs in company vehicles. Company uniforms (where provided) must be worn
  • Property: Company tools, vehicles, and equipment are to be used for work purposes only. Any damage must be reported immediately. Workers may be held liable for damage caused by gross negligence or deliberate misuse
  • Confidentiality: Client information, pricing, and commercially sensitive company information must not be shared with competitors or third parties without director approval
  • Social media: Workers must not post images of client properties, job sites, or colleagues on social media without written consent from the director and relevant parties
  • Mobile phones: Personal mobile phone use is not permitted while operating machinery, climbing, or driving. Phones may be used during designated break periods

Disciplinary Process

  • Breaches of this code will be addressed through the company's disciplinary procedure in accordance with the Employment Relations Act 2000
  • Serious misconduct (including dishonesty, harassment, intoxication at work, or endangering others) may result in summary dismissal without prior warnings
  • Workers have the right to have a support person present at any disciplinary meeting
16 Workplace Harassment & Bullying Prevention

Urban Tree Services Ltd is committed to a workplace free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination. All workers have the right to be treated with dignity and respect.

  • Harassment includes unwanted behaviour related to a person's gender, race, ethnicity, age, disability, sexual orientation, religion, or any other protected ground under the Human Rights Act 1993 — whether verbal, physical, or digital
  • Bullying is repeated, unreasonable behaviour directed at a worker or group of workers that creates a risk to health and safety. This includes intimidation, exclusion, humiliation, and excessive criticism
  • Sexual harassment — making unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favours, or engaging in behaviour of a sexual nature that is offensive, humiliating, or intimidating — is a serious breach of this policy and may constitute a criminal offence
  • Banter and workplace humour that a reasonable person would find offensive, humiliating, or demeaning is not acceptable, even if intended as a joke

Reporting

  • Workers who experience or witness harassment or bullying should report it to the director (Josh Micallef) as soon as possible
  • All complaints will be taken seriously, investigated promptly, and treated with confidentiality to the extent possible
  • Workers must not victimise or disadvantage anyone who raises a complaint in good faith
  • Substantiated harassment or bullying will result in disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal
  • Workers may also contact WorkSafe NZ (0800 030 040) or the Human Rights Commission (0800 496 877) if they feel their concerns are not being addressed
